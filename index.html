Occlusion-Aware window segmentation with conditional adversarial training guided by structural prior information
Manuela F. Cerón Viveros
source code

This work propose OA-WinSeg: Occlusion-Aware window segmentation network, a unique approach that integrates image segmentation with adversarial generative training to address occlusions in façade images. OA-WinSeg conditions the generation of window segmentation masks on a rectangular boundary guidance, enconding prior structural information of the hidden window boundaries. Unlike other studies that generate irregular or blob-shape segments, OA-WinSeg generates highly rectangular window shapes by incorporating adversarial training, rectangular guidance and bidirectional convolution with 1D Kernels, minimizing the need for complex vectorization mechanisms. In contrast to previous research, the availability of occlusion labels in our simulated dataset allows to perform a new targeted evaluation of segmentation methods within occluded regions, specifically assessing the performance in occlusion handling.

Simulated dataset with synthetic occlusions for window segmentation
Manuela F. Cerón Viveros
source code

This work introduces a novel workflow for creating a simulated dataset with synthetic occlusions. This workflow allows configuring the percentage of occluded samples in the dataset, as well as constraints for setting the size and location of various occluding objects. Importantly, the approach is adaptable to various building styles, and image resolutions, allows the addition of new categories of occluding objects and enables the generation of datasets of any size.
